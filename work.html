Eye-on Supervision Helper

Project Github

Notes:

In college I worked as a life guard at a summer camp. Every week a new group of campers would come in. Safety in the pool meant learning their names and faces quickly, knowing their swim level, tracking how many should be in the pool, who had not shown up, as well as who had gone to the restroom and at what time. Additionally, I needed to know of medical concerns such as an allergy or diabetes. It was a lot to keep track of, and a slip-up could create a life or death situation.

Eye-on is the app my younger self needed. It has a photo of every child in the pool with a colored border indicating their swim level (red, yellow, or green). The restroom icon removes them to the restroom list with a time-stamp of when they left. Clicking on the name card reveals a "back side" with important medical information. Schedule and time info at the top helps with keeping track of when it is time to prepare campers to leave for their next class.

This was my first app where I branched off on my own in using CSS and React. My greatest challenge, and the thing I am most proud of here, was the challenge of keeping the view uncluttered while incorporating all of the necessary information. Originally the camper cards were much larger with more text, which I later swapped out for icons and color codes. The smaller card size was important in order to maximize how many cards are visible at once.

Activity Scheduler

Project Github

Notes:

I once had to help our school secretary with scheduling a week of activities at an outdoor camp for approximately 50 students. She chose the activities, fit them into time blocks, assigned staff, and then printed out a signup sheet for each one. Each evening we would spend a couple of hours going through the process of hanging up all of the sign-ups by time slot and then walk each student through filling up their schedule for the next day. It. was. chaos... Some students would forget to sign up, others would accidently choose 2 activities during the same time. There were last minute changes to be with friends that left a slew of white out and cross out marks.

The goal of this project is to make that process easier. Students names will display in red until they have filled their schedule. Once an activity is chosen for a particular time block, it will not be possible to interact with any of the other activity choices during that time. Supervising staff will be able to receive a clean and easy to read form of their schedule and assigned students for the day.

This project currently is designed with HTML, CSS, and React

Centenial Celebration Logo

I was approached by the Alumni Association of the school I was working at previously. They were preparing for their centenial celebration and were wanting a logo that would be reflective of the school as it changed over the years. The wavy circle is a replica of the first logo, the pines are sung about in the school song, the bell was a special gift that sits at center campus, and the eagle later became the school mascott. In 2016 the school suffered a fire, and repairs brought the building to how it looks today - with a peaked roof in place of the original flat one. It was a fun project to work on, particularly because of the challenge of bringing the many different pieces together in a way that many generations of students could appreciate.
